How is this scored?

The final rating is a simple equal-weight average of all 12 criteria. No criterion counts more than any other — each one is worth exactly the same amount toward the final number.

Formula:
Final Rating = (sum of all 12 scores) ÷ 12

The result is rounded to the nearest quarter-star (0.25 increments) and expressed on a 1–5 scale.

This means a 5 in Prose Style and a 5 in Reading Experience pull the average equally. If you care more about one criterion than another, let that influence how carefully you score it — but the math treats them the same.

The final number is also translated into a plain-English descriptor: Extraordinary (4.75+), Excellent (4.25+), Very Good (3.75+), Good (3.25+), Mixed (2.75+), Below Average (2.25+), Weak (1.75+), or Poor.

All criteria default to 3 (Mixed / Adequate) when you open the tool. Adjust only the ones you feel strongly about, and the ones you leave at 3 will act as a neutral baseline.
